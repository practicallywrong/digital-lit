# Task 3: Coding & Collaboration Platforms – Reflection

## Part A – Coding Practice Platform

**Platform Used:** CodeChef

**Challenge Completed:** Arithmetic Operators

I created an account on CodeChef and completed the 
"Arithmetic Operators" beginner challenge. The problem required 
reading two integers and printing their sum — a simple 
but effective introduction to how competitive programming 
platforms work. After submitting the correct solution, 
I received a completion confirmation, which is included 
as a screenshot in this folder.

## Part B – Google Workspace Collaboration

**Tool Used:** Google Forms + Google Sheets

I created a 5-question "Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz" 
using Google Forms. The quiz is designed to test basic 
digital literacy knowledge among first-year students.

### Questions included:
1. What is digital literacy?
2. Which of the following is a safe internet practice?
3. Which tool is commonly used by students for online collaboration?
4. What should you do if you receive a suspicious email asking for personal information?
5. Why is maintaining a professional online presence important?

The form is linked to a Google Sheet that automatically 
collects responses, which is also screenshotted in this folder.

## How These Tools Will Help Academically

CodeChef will help me practice coding problems 
regularly and prepare for technical interviews and 
competitive programming contests in later years. 
Google Forms and Sheets are essential collaboration 
tools I will use for surveys, quizzes, and group 
project data collection throughout my degree. 
Getting familiar with Google Workspace early gives 
me a strong foundation for both academic and 
professional work.
