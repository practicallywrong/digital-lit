## What Each Platform Is Used For

**GitHub** is a platform for hosting and sharing code. 
Developers use it to showcase projects, contribute to 
open source, and collaborate with others. As a CSE 
student, this will be my primary portfolio platform.

**ResearchGate** is an academic networking platform 
used by researchers, students, and professionals to 
share research papers, ask questions, and collaborate 
on academic work. It helps in building a research 
profile and staying updated with developments in 
various fields.

**Stack Overflow** is a question-and-answer platform 
for programmers. Developers use it to ask doubts, 
share knowledge, and solve coding problems. It is one 
of the most valuable resources for learning practical 
programming and debugging issues.

## How I Plan to Use Them Over the Next Four Years

- **GitHub** – upload all projects, assignments, and 
  open source contributions semester by semester
- **ResearchGate** – explore research papers, connect 
  with researchers, and possibly publish academic work
- **Stack Overflow** – actively ask and answer 
  programming questions to improve problem-solving 
  skills and build a strong developer profile

These three platforms together form the foundation of 
my professional digital identity as a CSE student.
